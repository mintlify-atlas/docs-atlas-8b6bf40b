title: "Your Neo Letter profile and stats"
description: "Every signed-in Neo Letter player has a public profile page showing games played, wins, and total points. Learn how stats are tracked and updated."
---

Every Neo Letter account has a public profile page at `/profile/{username}`. Anyone can visit a profile by navigating to that URL — no account is required to view it. The page shows your profile picture (sourced from your Google account), your username, and the three stats that Neo Letter tracks for signed-in players.

<Note>
  Stats are only recorded for signed-in users. Games played as a guest are not counted toward any profile. Sign in before joining a room if you want your results tracked.
</Note>

## Stats

| Stat | Description |
|---|---|
| **Games Played** | Incremented once per completed game. A game is considered complete when the current round reaches the end of the answer list. |
| **Wins** | Incremented by 1 when you finish first place (highest total points) in a completed multiplayer game. Solo mode games do not count toward wins. |
| **Total Points** | Running sum of all points earned across every game. Points from each game are added to this total when the game ends. |

Stats are displayed using compact number formatting — for example, `1,400` is shown as `1.4K`.

## Profile picture

Your profile picture is the photo associated with your Google account at the time you signed up. It is stored as a URL in your Firestore profile document and is not hosted by Neo Letter directly. If you update your Google profile photo, the change will not be reflected automatically.

## Settings

A **Settings** link is visible on your own profile page at `/profile/{username}/settings`. The settings page is only accessible to the account owner — other users will see a "Not Authorized" message if they navigate to it directly. From settings you can delete your profile, which permanently removes your Firestore document and all associated stats.

## Finding a profile

Profiles are looked up by exact username match, and the lookup is case-sensitive. To find a player's profile, navigate to `/profile/{username}` with the exact username as it appears in the game. There is no fuzzy search or directory listing.
